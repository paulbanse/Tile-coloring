Welcome to this study about scientific practices. We want to find out under which circumstances scientific decisions are made.

You will play the role of a scientist whose job it is to conduct studies. You can conduct as many studies as you like and test as many subjects as you like in each study.

In scientific work, these decisions have consequences: Investing more effort in a single study increases the likelihood of a true and significant result (e.g. a difference between two groups of participants or a correlation between skills). On the other hand, spending more effort on a single study means that fewer resources are available for further studies with similar or different questions.

Welcome to this study. We are interested in how quickly people click with their mouse and/or keyboard depending on different targets

Feel free to score as many points as you can.

On the next page, you will play a simple task in which you use the mouse to color in tiles. Each time you successfully color in a tile, you will receive a point. During this study we ask you at different stages to either:

  • color many tiles in one color
  • use many different colors
  • follow a certain rule on the number of tiles in each color
Please carefully read what is required at each task.
